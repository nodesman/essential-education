## Purpose

This repository serves as the central hub for developing a series of books and related content focused on understanding complex human behaviors and strategic thinking. Key themes include identifying harm, analyzing strategic actions (like the role of money and risk), and promoting clarity in understanding real-world situations.
The motivation for focusing on harmful people and behaviors stems from recognizing that harm exists on a wide spectrum, far beyond easily identifiable criminal acts. There's a vast space of destructive actions that can lead to severe, irreparable consequences – including loss of life, devastation to one's family, or permanent financial ruin that eliminates future opportunities – yet some of these actions might operate within legal or even socially accepted boundaries. Understanding this spectrum and learning to identify potentially harmful individuals and their tactics is therefore crucial for self-preservation, well-being, and navigating life effectively.
The primary goal is to provide a structured yet flexible environment to capture disparate thoughts and gradually refine them into coherent written works. This process itself is intended as an act of clarification, translating lived experience and insights into understandable lessons for others. Ultimately, the aim is to equip readers with the vocabulary and conceptual understanding to identify problematic behaviors and navigate life more strategically, contributing to a world where unethical actions are less likely to succeed.

Initial thoughts on Boundaries will be captured in `rough_notes/` and tagged for potential integration into various works or potentially forming the basis of a dedicated book later.

## Workflow: Capturing and Refining Ideas

1.  **Capture Raw Thoughts:** Whenever an idea strikes, create a **new Markdown file** in the `rough_notes/` directory.
    *   Use a clear naming convention, like `YYYY-MM-DD_brief_topic.md` (e.g., `2023-10-27_idea_about_financial_leverage.md`).
    *   **Mark notes clearly:** Start the file content with `# ROUGH NOTE: [Brief Topic]` to explicitly state its status.
    *   Write freely. Don't worry about perfection, structure, or where it fits yet. Just get the thought down.

2.  **Tagging for Potential Placement (Optional but Recommended):** Since ideas might apply to multiple books or chapters, add simple tags or notes *within* the rough note file itself. This helps later when sorting.
    *   Example: Near the end of the note, you could add:
        ```
        Potential Placement:
        - [Book: Harmful People] Chapter: Manipulation Tactics
        - [Book: Strategic Actions] Concept: Resource Control
        - [Book: Strategic Actions] Chapter: Money as a Tool
        ```
    *   This allows a single raw idea to be mentally (and later physically) linked to multiple destinations without duplicating it prematurely.

3.  **Review and Process:** Periodically (e.g., weekly), review the files in `rough_notes/`.
    *   **Refine:** Clarify the writing, add detail, or break down complex ideas into smaller notes if needed.
    *   **Categorize & Relocate:**
        *   If a note is developing into specific book content, *move* or *copy* (and adapt) it into the relevant Markdown file within the `books/` directory structure. Update or remove the `ROUGH NOTE` marker.
        *   If a note is more suited for deeper personal reflection, synthesis of ideas, recording observations/experiments, or collecting wisdom, *move* or *copy* it to the `wisdom_and_analysis/` directory for further development or personal record-keeping.
